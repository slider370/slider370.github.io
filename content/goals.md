My long-term career objective is to work in **silicon engineering**, with a particular interest in **design verification**. I am drawn to verification because it sits at the intersection of logic design, systems thinking, and disciplined debugging. Verification engineers are responsible for proving that designs work under normal operation and under edge conditions, and that responsibility has a direct impact on product quality before tapeout.

In the near term, I am focused on three immediate goals:

1. **Pass my classes with strong fundamentals** in digital logic, computer architecture, and hardware/software interfaces.
2. **Secure an internship** where I can apply coursework in a professional engineering environment.
3. **Finish my FFT analyzer project** to strengthen my end-to-end project experience from architecture and implementation through validation and presentation.

## Timeline and Action Plan

### Now through next semester

- Keep building technical depth in classes that support RTL design and hardware verification.
- Continue active involvement in SiliconJackets to gain practical exposure to design, DV, and physical design collaboration.
- Turn project work into clear artifacts (documentation, results summaries, and reproducible scripts) that can be discussed in interviews.

### Next 12 months

- Apply broadly for hardware and verification-focused internships.
- Strengthen familiarity with verification workflows, including testbench structure, coverage thinking, and debugging methodology.
- Complete and polish technical project pages in this portfolio so they clearly communicate technical decisions and outcomes.

My involvement in SiliconJackets is part of this plan because it exposes me to how chip projects are organized from design through verification and physical implementation. Seeing the tapeout process at a student-team level helps me connect classroom topics to the standards and collaboration style used in real silicon projects.

![SiliconJackets tapeout visual that motivates my long-term silicon path](/images/sj-tapeout1.png)

### During junior and senior years

- Take advanced coursework that supports digital design and verification.
- Seek larger-scope team projects where I can contribute to module-level ownership and integration.
- Build a portfolio that demonstrates both execution quality and growth in engineering judgment.

### Long-term (post-graduation)

- Start in a role aligned with design verification or closely related silicon development work.
- Continue developing both depth and breadth so I can contribute across architecture, implementation, and verification boundaries.

This path is intentionally practical: improve fundamentals, gain hands-on experience, deliver complete projects, and use each stage to prepare for increasingly technical silicon work.
